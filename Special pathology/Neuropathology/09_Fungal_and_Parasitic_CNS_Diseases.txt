==============================================================
FUNGI
==============================================================
General:
  - Usually dissemination of systemic mycosis in IMMUNOSUPPRESSED animals
  - Rare: aspergillus, candida, mucor, skin fungi, or blue-green algae
  - More often: Coccidioides immitis, Blastomyces dermatitidis, Histoplasma capsulatum, Cryptococcus neoformans

Portal of entry:
  - Within macrophages (leukocyte monitoring)
  - Hematogenous route
  - Lead to granulomatous to pyogranulomatous inflammation

Macro: well-limited yellow-brown focal lesions

--- CRYPTOCOCCOSIS (Cryptococcus neoformans) ---
Species affected: cats, dogs, horses, cattle
Entry: direct spread after sinus or nasal infection, OR within macrophages

Virulence mechanisms:
  1. Thick protective MUCOPOLYSACCHARIDE CAPSULE
  2. Special metabolic pathway protecting against oxidative injury

Macro: within CNS and leptomeninges — multiple small cysts of GELATINOUS appearance

Micro:
  - Multicellular to monocellular fungi
  - CATS: without inflammatory reaction (or with mild/moderate infiltration of neutrophils, eosinophils, epithelioid cells, giant cells, lymphocytes)
  - DOGS, HORSES, CATTLE: mild to moderate inflammatory infiltration

Associated risk factors:
  - Prior or simultaneous infection with FIV, FeLV, Ehrlichia canis
  - Long-term glucocorticoid therapy

Histochemistry:
  - Fungi: spherical, half-moon shaped capsule
  - On H&E preparations: colourless, foamy appearance

==============================================================
PROTOZOA
==============================================================

--- TOXOPLASMOSIS (Toxoplasma gondii) ---
Definitive host: CAT (sexual replication and oocyst shedding)
Intermediate hosts: fish, amphibians, reptiles, birds, humans, and other mammals

CNS lesions occur in:
  - In utero infections
  - Adult immunocompromised individuals

Pathogenesis:
  1. TACHYZOITES infect and destroy endothelial cells
     → vasculitis → haemorrhagic infarcts and oedema
  2. Strong inflammatory response and neuronal damage:
     - Type III and IV hypersensitivity
     - Cytotoxic T lymphocytes

Macro:
  - Acute: bleeding, necrosis
  - Chronic: yellow-brown necrotic foci

Micro:
  ACUTE: swelling of endothelial cells, infarctions, oedema, tachyzoites within endothelium
  SUBACUTE: necrosis, often extensive bleeding, lymphocytic and macrophage perivascular infiltrates, astrogliosis, microgliosis
  CHRONIC: cysts with BRADYZOITES (often without major lesions if initial inflammatory response was effective)

Staining: visible with H&E; specifically visualised by immunohistochemical staining

--- NEOSPOROSIS (Neospora caninum) ---
Definitive host: DOGS
Intermediate hosts: dogs, cats, cattle, sheep, horses

Entry: not clearly established — possibly transplacental with macrophages

Location within CNS: protozoa found in neurons, ependymal cells, mononuclear cells, and endothelial cells

Injury mechanism: endothelial cell damage

CNS diseases:
  - In utero infections (mid-to-late pregnancy): abortions in cattle
  - Adult animal infections: dogs and horses

Macro and micro: similar to toxoplasmosis
Key difference: cystic developmental forms of N. caninum noted ONLY IN THE BRAIN (not other organs)

==============================================================
MULTICELLULAR PARASITES
==============================================================

--- INSECT LARVAE ---
SHEEP — Oestrus ovis:
  - Nasal cavity → ethmoidal bone → cranium → CNS damage

CATTLE — Hypoderma bovis:
  - Larval migration into the spinal canal
  - CNS damage usually occurs AFTER ANTHELMINTIC THERAPY
  - (Inflammatory reaction to dead parasites causes the damage)

--- TAPEWORMS / CESTODES ---
COENUROSIS:
  - Agent: Coenurus cerebralis (larva of dog tapeworm Taenia multiceps)
  - Host: sheep (intermediate host); dogs (definitive host)
  - Enters hematogenously → brain → large cyst filled with fluid → COMPRESSIVE ATROPHY

NEUROCYSTICERCOSIS:
  - Agent: Cysticercus cellulosae (larval form of human tapeworm Taenia solium)
  - Intermediate carrier: pig, also dog
  - Forms round cysts in the brain

--- CEREBROSPINAL NEMATODIASIS ---
Definition: aberrant migration of larval nematode parasites into or through the CNS

Common species:
  - Strongylus spp. (horse)
  - Baylisascaris procyonis (dog)
  - Dirofilaria immitis (dog)
  - Halicephalobus deletrix (horse)
  - Toxocara canis (dog)

Entry into CNS: hematogenic route

Mechanism of injury: damage to endothelium due to larval migration

Macro: LINEAR OR SNAKE-LIKE TRACES of necrosis and bleeding in CNS tissue

Micro: visible larvae, mononuclear and eosinophilic encephalitis
