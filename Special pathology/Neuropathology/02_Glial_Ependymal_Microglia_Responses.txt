==============================================================
ASTROCYTE RESPONSES TO INJURY
==============================================================

ASTROCYTOSIS
  - Increase in cell number and cell volume

ASTROGLIOSIS
  - Cell hypertrophy due to intensified synthesis of intermediate filaments
  - Increase in length and complexity of branching astrocytic processes
  - Swelling = acute reversible response
  - Permanent stimulation leads to hypertrophy

GEMYSTOCYTES
  - Astrocytes with eccentric nucleus and abundant pink (eosinophilic) cytoplasm
  - Unlike the "invisible cytoplasm" of healthy astrocytes
  - Occur in diseases with long-term disruption of balance between intracellular and extracellular fluid

ALZHEIMER TYPE II ASTROCYTES
  - Astrocytic nuclei in pairs with pronounced nucleoli surrounded by a clear bright space — ring of oedematous cytoplasm
  - Present in large numbers in individuals with HEPATIC ENCEPHALOPATHY

==============================================================
OLIGODENDROCYTE RESPONSES TO INJURY
==============================================================

SATELLITOSIS
  - Swelling and hypertrophy of perineuronal oligodendrocytes
  - Cell swelling, hypertrophy, degeneration: perineuronal and interfascicular oligodendrocytes
  - Proliferation and replacement of degenerated cells: exclusive precursor cells of oligodendrocytes

PRIMARY DEMYELINATION
  - Interfascicular cells degenerate under: ischemia, viruses, lead poisoning, autoimmune diseases
  - Axon remains intact
  - Protracted demyelination → irreversible atrophy of neurons
  - Precursor cells located in subventricular zone → proliferation-differentiation to oligodendroglia cells → REMYELINATION

SECONDARY DEMYELINATION
  - Axonal injury leads to myelin loss = secondary demyelination + necrosis of neurons
  - Phagocytosis and removal of myelin lamellae and dissolution of oligodendroglia (CNS) or Schwann cells (PNS)
  - Resembles Wallerian degeneration

==============================================================
EPENDYMAL CELLS AND CHOROID PLEXUS EPITHELIAL CELLS AT INJURY
==============================================================

ATROPHY (compressive) AND DEGENERATION
  - Increased chamber volume — hydrocephalus
  - Loss of microvilli and cilia
  - Reduction of cell organelles

NECROSIS
  - Ventricular enlargement, stretching, and tearing of the ependymal lining
  - Direct exposure of the CNS to CSF
  - THERE IS NO REGENERATION of ependymal cells
  - Denuded areas remain permanently

==============================================================
MICROGLIA RESPONSE TO INJURY
==============================================================
  - Tissue injury activates them → become immunocompetent
  - Secrete cytokines, chemokines, nitric oxide (NO), reactive oxygen species (ROS)
  - Together with astrocytes and neurons: coordinate inflammatory processes
  - The response range is proportional to the severity of the injury
  - Hypertrophy and hyperplasia
  - Phagocytosis: myelin, cellular detritus, neurons (NEURONOPHAGIA)

==============================================================
MENINGES RESPONSE TO INJURY
==============================================================
Routes of infection:
  - Hematogenic infection
  - By axonal transport
  - Transmission within leukocytes

MENINGITIS
  - Inflammation of leptomeninges (arachnoidea + pia mater)
  - Exudates and inflammatory cells accumulate in subarachnoid space
  - Can be: acute, subacute, or chronic
  - Types: suppurative, nonsuppurative, granulomatous, eosinophilic

PACHYMENINGITIS
  - Inflammation of the dura mater
  - Inflammation of external (periosteal layer) of dura mater — consequence of osteomyelitis
  - Formation of extradural abscesses
  - Consequence of skull fracture
  - Spread of leptomeningitis to internal surface of the dura
  - Senile changes — mineralisation and ossification of the spinal cord dura

DURAL OSSIFICATION / OSSIFICATING PACHYMENINGITIS (= Dural Metaplasia)
  - Senile change in large breed dogs
  - Cervical and lumbar regions
  - Formation of bone plates with bone marrow
  - Ventrolateral surface
  - Bone plates compress spinal cord nerve roots → pain on flexion and extension of the spine
