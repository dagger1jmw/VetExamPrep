==============================================================
PRION DISEASES (TRANSMISSIBLE SPONGIFORM ENCEPHALOPATHIES — TSE)
==============================================================
Diseases:
  - Ovine spongiform encephalopathy (scrapie)
  - Bovine spongiform encephalopathy (BSE)
  - Feline spongiform encephalopathy
  - Chronic wasting disease (deer)
  - Transmissible mink encephalopathy
  - Exotic ungulate encephalopathy

Mechanism:
  - Accumulation of abnormal isoforms (beta-pleated sheet conformation) of otherwise normal cellular protein: PRION PROTEIN (PrP)
  - Post-translational modification of the normal prion protein occurs

Pathology:
  - NO macroscopic changes
  - Histo: neuronal degeneration with cytoplasmic vacuoles = SPONGIFORM CHANGE + ASTROCYTOSIS

==============================================================
DEGENERATIVE DISEASES — METABOLIC
==============================================================

--- HEPATIC ENCEPHALOPATHY ---
Cause: liver failure (acute, chronic, or atrophy) → failure of ornithine urea cycle → accumulation of toxic substances in bloodstream

Key toxic substance: AMMONIA (NH3)

Mechanisms of CNS damage:
  - Alterations of amino acid turnover
  - Disruption of water and electrolyte transport through neuronal membranes
  - Inhibition of excitatory and inhibitory postsynaptic potential generation
  - Increased permeability of hematoencephalic barriers → entry of neurotoxins
  - Disturbed osmoregulation within the CNS

Micro:
  - Spongiform change (intramyelin oedema, fragmentation, and vacuolisation of myelin membrane)
  - Development of ALZHEIMER TYPE II ASTROCYTES

==============================================================
DEGENERATIVE DISEASES — NUTRITIVE
==============================================================

--- VITAMIN B1 (THIAMINE) DEFICIENCY ---
Role of thiamine: cofactor of several enzymes involved in carbohydrate (CH) metabolism

Mechanism of brain damage:
  - Reduced enzyme activity → lack of energy
  - Oxidative stress → reactive oxygen species (ROS)
  - Thiamine essential in synthesis of neurotransmitters

Most sensitive species: ruminants and dogs

RUMINANTS — POLIOENCEPHALOMALACIA (Cerebrocortical Necrosis):
  - Caused by thiamine deficiency (or bacterial/enzymatic degradation of thiamine in rumen)
  - Macro: brain swelling, yellowish discoloration of cerebrocortical grey matter
    * Autofluorescence
    * Oedematous separation and necroses at the grey-white matter interface
    * Prominent areas of atrophy of gyri
    * Thinning or absence of grey matter
  - Micro: neuronal degeneration and necrosis, spongiosis, myelin degeneration

CARNIVORES:
  - Macro: bilateral symmetrical malacia of brain stem nuclei
  - Micro: neuronal degeneration and necrosis, spongiosis, myelin degeneration

DEGENERATIVE DISEASES — TOXICOSES
==============================================================

--- CLOSTRIDIUM PERFRINGENS TYPE D ENCEPHALOPATHY ---
Species: sheep, goats, cattle
Toxin: EPSILON TOXIN

Mechanism:
  - Vascular injury and failure of hematoencephalic barrier
  - Epsilon toxin binds to endothelial cells
  - Opens tight junctions
  - Increased vascular permeability
  - → Vasogenic oedema → swelling of astrocytic processes → necrosis

Macro:
  - Bilaterally symmetrical foci of ENCEPHALOMALACIA
  - Extra-neural changes: congestion and pulmonary oedema, hydropericardium, petechiae, PULPY KIDNEY

Micro:
  - Vasogenic oedema
  - Hyalinisation of capillary walls
  - Endothelial cell swelling
  - Pericapillary bleeding
  - Acute necrosis of neurons and macroglial cells

--- SODIUM CHLORIDE (SALT) INTOXICATION ---
Species: pigs, poultry; occasionally dogs, horses, sheep
Context: animals deprived of water and then given unlimited access; also important role of NaCl quantity in food

Mechanism:
  - HYPEROSMOLARITY develops (sodium accumulates in brain)
  - Sudden shift to HYPOOSMOLARITY (water access)
  - Water suddenly enters the brain → oedema
  - Brain unable to adapt fast enough
  - Cannot resolve pre-accumulated ions and osmotically active molecules

Macro: oedematous brain and meningeal congestion

Micro:
  - CEREBROCORTICAL LAMINAR NEURONAL NECROSIS
  - Astrocytic swelling
  - Frequent infiltration of EOSINOPHILS and macrophages

--- OEDEMA DISEASE (ENTEROTOXEMIC COLIBACILLOSIS) ---
Agent: E.coli strains producing SHIGA-LIKE TOXIN TYPE IIe (verotoxin)
Species: pigs, 4–8 weeks of age

Mechanism:
  - Toxin causes necrosis of smooth muscle cells of small arteries and arterioles
  - → Focal reduction of blood circulation through CNS
  - → Infarction = ANGIOPATHY

Macro (extra-neural): swelling of specific localities, transudation into body cavities

Macro (brain): bilaterally symmetric focal necrosis (yellow-grey, soft, lytic) within the CAUDAL MEDULLA OBLONGATA

Micro (medulla oblongata and meninges):
  - Perivascular oedema at early stage
  - Necrosis of smooth muscles of the tunica media
  - Retention of fibrinogenic material
  - Accumulation of macrophages and lymphocytes within adventitia
  - Degenerative angiopathy (spongiosis, vasogenic oedema, necrosis of neurons and glia cells)
  - NOTE: Preserved endothelium and NO thrombosis

==============================================================
DEGENERATIVE DISEASES — STORAGE DISEASES
==============================================================

--- LYSOSOMAL STORAGE DISEASES ---
Definition: disorder of lysosomal-mediated degradation of substrates of normal cell metabolism
  - Substrates cannot be degraded in lysosomes → accumulation → death of affected cell
  - Causes: various mutations affecting the metabolism of these substances
  - Result: increase of neuronal cell body size (visible microscopically)
