CELL TYPES OF THE CNS
The CNS contains five major cell types:
  - Neurons
  - Glial cells
  - Endothelial cells
  - Meninges (collagen and elastic fibres, fibrocytes)
  - Blood vessel pericytes
  - Ependymal cells

==============================================================
1. NEURONS
==============================================================
Function:
  - Accepting, transferring, and delivering of impulses
  - Transmission of electrical and chemical impulses
  - Local and temporal interpretation of impulses
  - Inhibitory and stimulatory regulation of impulses

Types:
  - Afferent (sensory)
  - Efferent (motor)
  - Interneurons — interneuronal communication within the CNS

==============================================================
2. GLIAL CELLS
==============================================================
Glia = "glue"
10x more glial cells than neurons.

Origin:
  - From neuroectoderm: astrocytes, oligodendroglia, and ependymal cells
  - From mesoderm (bone marrow): microglia

a) ASTROCYTES
  - Protoplasmatic type (Type I) — grey matter
  - Fibrous type (Type II) — white matter

  Functions:
    - Regulation of concentrations of extracellular neurotransmitters and balance of fluid/electrolytes
    - Correction of injury (loss of tissue substance) by proliferation of astrocytic cell strains (analogous to fibroblasts)
    - Supporting and bonding bundles of functionally linked axons passing through the CNS
    - Participation in the barrier system

b) OLIGODENDROGLIA
  - Interfascicular oligodendrocytes: myelination of axons
  - Satellite oligodendrocytes: regulation of perineuronal microenvironment; on disorders respond by proliferation = SATELLITOSIS

c) EPENDYMAL CELLS
  - Line the ventricular system
  - Choroid plexus epithelial cells: modified ependymal cells that line the choroid plexus (capillary network protruding into the ventricular system)
    * Create CSF
    * Function as Blood-CSF barrier
    * Prominent brush border (microvilli)

MICROGLIA
  - Belongs to the monocytic-macrophage system
  - Functions: immunosurveillance, immunoregulation, and phagocytosis

==============================================================
3. MENINGES
==============================================================
From outside to inside: dura mater → arachnoidea → pia mater
  - Subarachnoid space filled with CSF
  - Function: structural/functional barrier; participates in amortization of head trauma

==============================================================
4. ENDOTHELIAL CELLS AND HEMATOENCEPHALIC BARRIER (Blood-Brain Barrier)
==============================================================
Barrier consists of:
  - Endothelial cells of the capillary
  - Basement membrane
  - Astrocyte foot processes

Very expressed (tight) junctions between endothelial cells.
Very selective molecule transport.

Active regulation:
  - Selective regulation of extracellular space
  - Isolation from sudden biochemical changes in peripheral circulation
  - Active regulation of transport of proteins and hydrophilic molecules
  - Diffusion of small lipid molecules
  - Receptor transport: insulin, transferrin, glucose, purine, amino acids
  - Negative charge of the basement membrane prevents entry of anionic molecules

Sub-anatomical details:
  - In several areas, pia mater = one cell layer with fenestrated capillaries
  - Arteries penetrating cerebral cortex enter pia mater and glia limitans, then disappear as they transform into capillaries
  - Pia mater and glia limitans separate CNS from subarachnoid space
  - Ependymal barrier (CSF barrier): barrier between CSF and brain — more permeable than other barriers; formed by ependymal cells and astrocytic processes

==============================================================
GREY MATTER vs WHITE MATTER
==============================================================
GREY MATTER:
  - Cell bodies of neurons (soma)
  - Oligodendrocytes/oligodendroglia (perineuronal satellite cells)
  - Protoplasmatic astrocytes
  - Microglia
  - Neuropil: network of thin myelinated axons, dendrites, glial cells (oligodendroglia and astrocyte) processes and synapses

WHITE MATTER:
  - Network of well-myelinated axons
  - Oligodendrocytes (interfascicular)
  - Fibrous astrocytes
  - Microglia
