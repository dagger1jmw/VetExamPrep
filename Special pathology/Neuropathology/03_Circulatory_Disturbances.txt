SEQUEL TO THE DEVELOPMENT OF LESIONS (General Pathway):
  Endothelial dysfunction
  → vasoactive mediators
  → local and systemic changes in blood flow, pressure and permeability increase
  → bacterial toxins increase damage
  → vasculitis, bleeding, DIC, ischemia
  → infarction
  → vasogenic oedema

==============================================================
INFARCTION
==============================================================
Definition: necrosis due to arteriolar obstruction

FAST ISCHEMIA (e.g. emboli):
  - Extensive tissue lesion
  - Neuronal necrosis: within seconds/minutes
  - Necrosis of other structures: within hours

SLOW ISCHEMIA (e.g. atherosclerosis):
  - Development of anastomoses (partial compensation)

CAUSES OF CEREBRAL ISCHEMIC NECROSIS:
  - Interrupted circulation in the brain — after heart attack (cardiac arrest)
  - Sudden hypotension — caused by reduction of heart rate
  - Drop in oxygen concentration in inhaled air
  - Methemoglobinemia — CO (carbon monoxide) poisoning
  - Cyanide intoxication — inhibition of cellular respiration

EXTENT OF ISCHEMIC NECROSIS (STROKE):
  - Greater than the area of vascularisation covered by the obstructed artery
  - Reason: degenerated neurons in the necrotic centre release neurotransmitter GLUTAMATE
    → damage of healthy neurons at the edge of necrosis via EXCITOTOXICITY

HAEMORRHAGIC INFARCTION:
  - More devastating effect than pale (ischemic) infarction
  - Compressive atrophy and necrosis of surrounding brain due to blood accumulation in necrotic tissue

MACROSCOPIC APPEARANCE:
  - Liquefactive necrosis = tissue softening; abundance of lipids and enzymes
  - Grey matter infarction = HAEMORRHAGIC
  - White matter infarction = PALE (ischemic) — small number of anastomoses

REPAIR:
  - Lack of fibrous connective tissue stroma in CNS
  - Small infarcts → reparation with astrogliosis
  - Large infarcts → cyst formation

==============================================================
CONGESTIVE BRAIN SWELLING
==============================================================
  - Post-traumatic brain enlargement
  - Cause: elevated intracranial pressure, uncontrolled vasodilation, increased total volume of blood in distended brain capillaries
  - Mechanism:
      Increased hydrostatic pressure
      → leakage of fluid into extravascular spaces
      → increase in cellular fluid = intracellular swelling
