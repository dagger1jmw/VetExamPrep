AZOTEMIA

• Urine of dogs and cats contains only a small amount of protein
• Selective permeability of the glomerular capillary wall restricts the filtration of most plasma proteins on the basis of protein weight and charge
• 60,000 – 65.000 daltons
• The negatively charged glomerular capillary wall impedes the pasage of negatively charged proteins (albumin)
• Proteins that do pass can be resorbed in proximal tubular epithelial cells

= increased concentrations of urea and creatinine in the blood
• Urea is synthesized in the liver from ammonia which is generated from catabolism of endogenous and ingested proteins - It is excreted by kidneys
• Creatinine is irreversibly formed by nonenzymatic metabolism in the muscle
-> Its production is proportional to muscle mass - Not affected by ingested protein because the production is constant
=> increase in serum creatinine concentration is indicative of decreased renal excretion
