-> weak pulse - hypokinetic : dilated cardiomyopathy, (sub)aortic stenosis, shock, dehydration
-> strong pulse : excitement, hypertrophic cardiomyopathy, hyperthyroidism, fever
-> very strong pulse - hyperkinetic : patent ductus arteriosus, severe aortic regurgitation, fever/sepsis

• normal breathing consists of three phases of equal lenght : inspiration, expiration and pause
• variation in the lenght of one or all phases constitutes rhythm abnormality
• prolongation of inspiration is usually due to obstruction of the upper respiratory tract, typically the larynx
• prolongation of expiration is usually due to disease of the lower respiratory tract
-> in lung diseases, the rhythm typically consists only of inspiration and expiration

RUMEN CONTRACTIONS
• rumination is the complex process of regurgitation, remastication, ensalivation, and reswallowing of reticulorumen contents
• requires a positive approach, and is easily stopped by fright or the offering of feed → determining the cause of failure to ruminate is a major diagnostic challenge
• the contractions of the stomach compartments are organized and controled through the vagus nerve and the ruminoreticular motor center in medulla oblongata
• the cyclical movements, which ensure thorough mixing of the contents of the rumen and reticulum occur at the rate of 1-3 per minute, the greater frequency being related to recent ingestion of feed
Examination of the rumen (no palpation)

Type

• normally both sides of the thorax move uniformly
• in painful diseases of the thorax and in pneumothorax, one side of the thorax may not move as much as the other
• the movements of the hemithoraxes are evaluated by looking down on the animal from above

• exposure to enviromental conditions that promote heat loss (cold, wet and windy)
• effects of hypothermia: shivering, hypotension, increased blood viscosity, cardiac arrhythmias, hypoxemia and acidosis
• lethal low body temperature in both dogs and humans is approximately 25ºC
• neonates are especially susceptible to hypothermia, particularly if ingestion of colostrum/milk is delayed

-> Inspection
• contractions of the rumen are visible by inspection of the left paralumbar fossa and the left lateral abdominal wall
• alternate rising and sinking of the left paralumbar fossa is associated with the concurrent contraction and relaxation of the dorsal and ventral sacs
• more frequent during the eating and rumination
• in normal animal, three contractions should occur over a 2-minute period; one of the contractions should be associated with eructation of the gas
-> Auscultation
• auscultation is the most sensitive of the three methods
• allows evaluation of ruminal functions, and to a varying degree that of the other stomach compartments
• stethoscope is placed in the left paralumbar fossa and extending cranially over an area from the dorsal third of the 10th to 13th ribs
=> rumen contractions are evaluated for: intensity, duration, and frequency
• they are influenced by:
➢ time after feeding
➢ recent excitement
➢ composition of the diet
➢ access to water supply

Sounds
-> normal sounds
• the sounds of rumen contractions in a normal animal consuming roughage are similar to a series of exploding/crackling/
grating sounds
• when the rumen contains less coarse roughage or primarily grain, the sounds may be less distinct but still possess a
crackling/grating characteristic
-> abnormal sounds
• a decrease in the intensity or the absence of ruminal sounds over the left paralumbar fossa and the area cranial to it
suggests presence of a viscus or mass between the rumen and the abdominal wall à most common cause is left-sided
displacement of the abomasum
-> common causes of reduction in rumen contractions : hypocalcemia, endotoxemia, simple indigestion, acute
carbohydrate engorgement, traumatic reticuloperitonitis
• presence of fluid-tinkling or splashing sounds, along with a static rumen, suggests that the rumen contains and excessive
quantity of fluid
-> this findings suggests diseases, such as grain overload, or an atonic rumen
-> sounds are similar to those present in left-sided displacement of the abomasum
-> in vagus indigestion the contractions are obvious and frequent, visible (and palpable), and usually not audible
-> complete atony may also occur in vagus indigestion
