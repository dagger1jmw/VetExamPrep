HISTORY TAKING TECHNIQUES
GENERALITIES
-> Anamnesis = memory, reminiscence = history = stoty of the patient
« It is widely taught that diagnosis is revealed in the patient's history » => Listen to your patient; they are telling you the diagnosis!"
-> Data that we collect during the conversation with the owner considering
- onset, course and progress of the disease
- signs / symptoms
- keeping conditions, diet , habits etc.

GOLDEN RULE OF HISTORY TAKING

HOW TO PROCEED ?
-> always ask general questions first
-> ensure that no valuable question is forgotten
-> getting started: general questions – methodical approach
- when was the pet last “normal?” and what was the first symptom?
- was there any action considering first symptoms (by the owner or vet)
- are the symptoms getting worse, getting better or neither?
- previous diseases? chronic diseases?
- treatment (current or previous)
- other animals in the house/farm...?
- recent changes in environment (recent changes in keeping? diet? arrival of new animal at the farm/in the house, house
- renovating/painting, deratization, detergents & chemicals)
-> take notes during the chat with the owner
gender? neutering status? heat cycle? partus? breeding? lactation?
-> after general questions ask anything that could guide you towards the correct diagnosis
=> on the basis of collected information create a list of differentials (individual for each patient)

1) Be a good listener : let the owners tell you “their” story
=> Do not interrupt them unless you have to
2) When owners stop talking, start asking them questions
-> This does not apply for energy patients
+ Other golden rules :
- Use simple language, avoid medical terms
- When in doubt, ask more (additional) questions
- Be emphathic, encourage the owner to tell you everything

WE CAN ALSO MAKE OUR LIFE EASIER :
-> Has advantages and disadvantages

⚠ And of course... TRUST NOONE
-> Learn how to select the right information - separate relevant from irrelevant
-> History might be “half of the diagnosis”, but it can also mislead us towards wrong conclusions

Correct diagnosis– correct choice
Correct choice– crucial informations
Crucial informations– right questions
=> Basis of clinical experience
