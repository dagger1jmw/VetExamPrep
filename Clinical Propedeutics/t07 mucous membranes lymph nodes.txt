-> FNA
- fine-needle aspiration and smear preparation occasionally provides diagnostic information
- BUT the sample is small and unlikely to be representative of the lesion, thus producing a ‘false negative’
=> it’s purpose is confirmation, not exclusion of a disease
- an exception is lymph node abscessation

-> incisional biopsy
- best option
- a wedge section is excised under local anaesthesia or if necessary, with sedation
- large sample retains better morphology and improves the chance of a definitive diagnosis
- any haemorrhage should be identified
- local swelling is usually minimal
- resolves in 5–7 days
=> most easily collected from a superficial lymph node
-> can be adequately immobilized against the overlying skin
- one half is submitted for bacterial culture and the other is put into formalin for histopathology
