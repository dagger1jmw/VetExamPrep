Hemoptysis
• Coughing up blood from lower respiratory tract (lungs)
• Blood usually bright red & frothy with air bubbles

• protective re ex initiated from cough center in the medulla
oblongata characterized by explosive expiration of air from the lungs,
following brief closure of the glottis, which results from irritation of
sensory receptors in the respiratory system distributed in so-called
tusigen zones, location: pharynx, larynx, trachea, bronchi (lat. tussis
= cough: terminal broncioles and lungs are not tusigen zones)
• Phases of cough: deep inspiration followed by closure of the
glottis vigorous & forced expiration (glottis still
closed) compression of the air in the lungs sudden opening of the
glottis: result: explosive expiration

Important
• Sudden large bleeding through nose = potentially
pulmonary hemorrhage —> can be fatal (horses/cattle)

• protective re ex characterized by sudden, forceful and brief
exhallation with purpose to clear nasopharynx induced by irritative
stimulation of the nasal mucosa (EXAMPLEs: smoke, gases, foreign
bodies, etc.).
• Normal: occasional sneezing, abnormal: persistent sneezing (local
disease process: eg. in ammation, tumor).

• protective re ex (mostly) in dogs characterized by spastic
inspiratory e ort with purpose to clear nasopharynx of irritants.
• Persistent reverse sneezing is a sign of nasopharingeal or paranasal
sinus disease process.
