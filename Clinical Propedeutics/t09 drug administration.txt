CLINICAL TIP
-> Intramuscular injection (IM)
- Preferred site: neck triangle injection site (between the scapula, cervical vertebrae, and nuchal ligament)
- Procedure :
1 Clap/tap the area before injection
2 Insert the needle fully first, then attach the syringe
3 Hold syringe and needle together during injection because thick products may separate them
4 Aspirate before injection, then inject
5 Remove syringe and needle together
6 Avoid gluteal muscles because of meat consumption/meat quality concerns

-> Subcutaneous injection (SC)
- Preferred site: skin cranial to the scapula
- Change needle each time because ruminant skin is thick
- Second choice: skin caudo-dorsal to the elbow
-> Intravenous injection (IV)
- Mainly in the jugular vein => lift the head slightly and turn it a little
- Mammary vein can sometimes be used
-> Catheter placement
- Usually placed in the jugular vein or ear veins
