14. Rectal Examination in Dogs

Secondary impaction = Occurs because another disease causes
dehydration/hypovolemia and dry colon contents.

When to perform it
In any adult dog presenting with:
• tenesmus : straining to defecate
• dyschezia : painful or di cult defecation
• constipation
• hematochezia : fresh blood in feces
• diarrhea
• dysuria : di cult or painful urination
• stranguria straining/painful urination, often with small amounts

Seen with:
• grass sickness
• anterior enteritis
• ileal impaction
Important exam point
• Do not mistake secondary impaction for primary impaction, because
giving large volumes of mineral oil by nasogastric tube may risk
